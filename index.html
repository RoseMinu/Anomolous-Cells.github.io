Project Snapshot

  • Goal: Identify anomalous cells inside nominally similar batches.
  • Inputs: EIS data at multiple states of charge (SOC).
  • Target: Rate capability & capacity-based performance.
  • Outcome: Models to flag cells likely to fail or underperform.

Project Overview

This project focuses on identifying anomalous battery cells within groups of cells that share similar chemistries and mass loading. Using impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measured at multiple states of charge, we explore which SOC is most predictive of a cellâ€™s eventual performance under rate capability tests.

The pipeline includes:

  1. Cleaning and organizing EIS data collected at different SOC levels.
  2. Exploratory analysis to understand trends and correlations.
  3. Feature engineering from EIS spectra and rate tests.
  4. Modeling to detect anomalous cells based on rate-capability performance.

Data

  • EIS measurements
    Multi-frequency impedance spectra at various states of charge (SOC).
  • Rate capability tests
    Capacity and voltage response at different C-rates to quantify performance.
  • Cell groups
    Cells with varying chemistries and mass loading, enabling cross-group comparison.

Methods

  • Exploratory analysis
    Correlating EIS features with rate capability to find the most predictive SOC.
  • Feature engineering
    Extraction of magnitude/phase features, characteristic frequencies, and summary statistics from Nyquist and Bode representations.
  • Modelling & anomaly detection
    Supervised and unsupervised approaches to flag cells with abnormal rate-capability behaviour relative to the group.

Key Findings (high-level)

Predictive SOC identification

EIS at specific SOC windows shows stronger correlation with rate capability, helping to prioritize which SOC to measure in future experiments.

Anomaly patterns

Anomalous cells exhibit distinct impedance signatures compared to the bulk, especially in low-frequency regions linked to transport limitations.

Practical impact

Early screening of cells enables removal or closer monitoring of outliers, improving overall pack reliability and experimental efficiency.
